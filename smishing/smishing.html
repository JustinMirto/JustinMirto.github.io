How to Spot Smishing Texts
Check the phone number or sender
If you are unsure if the phone number or the sender is legit, look up the number online. If it is a legitimate text, then the search results should come up with the company it is associated with.
What is the tone?
If the tone makes you feel like you need to act quickly, or you will face some sort of financial or legal situation there is a good chance that the text is a smishing attempt. An official, legitimate text from an institution would not make you feel like you need to panic, but rather try to ease you - they will never ask you to send any form of credentials or any sum of money.
Suspicious Links
A smishing text could potentially include a malicious link under the guise of being a legitimate link - for example, the person sending the text could claim clicking on the link will have a positive result such as helping a person in need. If you see a link and you suspect it to not be real, do not click on the link to stay safe.
