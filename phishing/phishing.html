How to Spot Phishing Emails
Spelling and punctuation
If you have received an email, make sure to read over its contents properly. An email claiming to be from a reputable company would potentially have basic grammar or punctuation mistakes - a legitimate email would typically not have any of these mistakes, and if they did it would most likely be a minor error.
Suspicious Links?
If you think a link is suspicious, don’t take the risk and click it. Instead, hover over the link and see what address comes up. Does the address match the link? If not, then most likely it is a phishing email - make sure to report the email as a phishing attempt!
Who sent it?
If you think you received an email that is trying carry out a phishing attack, take a look at who sent it. An email might come from someone claiming to be from Amazon, but the sender’s email might be Am4zon.com - this is a common trick when phishing emails are carried out!
Urgency
A common trick used is creating a false sense of urgency or danger. They will claim that you have to perform an action immediately, or you will face some form of legal or financial problem. They do this so you panic, stop thinking clearly (or stop thinking at all), and do what they want under pressure.
